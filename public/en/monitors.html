Tools for Measuring Noise

Noise data collection can be done many devices, including smartphones and commercial sound level meters.

Phone Apps

Below are recommendations for apps to measure decibel levels.

NIOSH Sound Level Meter App

NIOSH Sound Level Meter is an iOS app that was developed by the National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health. The app is free to download and is designed to measure sound levels in the workplace. The app provides a readout of the sound level using the built-in microphone on the device.
Learn more

Decibel X Pro

Decibel X Pro is an iOS and Android app that is designed to measure sound levels. The app provides a readout of the sound level using the built-in microphone on the device. The app costs $50 to download, or there is a free verison (Decibel X) with that allows for single, 30 second measurements.
Learn more

SPLnFFT Noise Meter

SPLnFFT is a $4 app available on for iOS devices. The app includes a frequency analyzer, frequency meter, test signal generator, and a dosimeter. It is recommended by the "Laborers' Health and Safety Fund of North America".
Learn more

Sound Meter and Noise Detector

Sound Meter and Noise Detector is a $3 app available on for Android devices. The app reports minimal, average, maximal decibel level and real-time graph.
Learn more

Measuring Decibel Level With Phones

When measuring with smartphones, it is important to hold the device and record properly for the most accurate data.

  • Hold the smartphone at about 10 - 12" (~25-30 cm) from your "hearing zone" (sphere around your head).
  • The microphone should be pointed directly at the source of noise, preferably at 30¬∞ - 45¬∞angle.
  • If the noise source is constant, you only need a 30 second to 1 minute measurement.
  • If the noise source is varying, longer periods of measurements are desired, to try to capture all the variations. At least 15 minutes of measuring is suggested.
  • Take precautions not to touch the microphone, tap, or rub with your fingers as this can introduce artifacts into the measurement.
  • Do not use in high wind conditions, use a windscreen if you are using an external microphone (some come equipped with windscreens).
  • Avoid measuring within 1 meter of large noise-emitting or reflecting surface.
  • If possible, calibrate the app using a commercial sound level meter for reference
